Almond

Boutique hospitality is opening its third door in Abuja — built, like the first two, from the ground up and chosen leaf by leaf.

ABUJA, NIGERIA  ·  OPENING SOON

The idea

"Branding isn't an afterthought here. The logo, the colors, the aesthetic — every detail is chosen on purpose."

Almond is the newest property from the team behind Yellow Door Hotels — a boutique stay shaped by the same instinct for craft that built the first two, just dressed in a different palette: warm, earthbound, almond-toned. Before a single guest checks in, the materials on site are being chosen by hand, down to the colour of the table linen.

What's taking shape

Still under construction —
here's what's coming.

Four parts of Almond, four colours from the same fan.

The Suites
A small set of rooms, each considered on its own terms — not a copy of the one next door.
The Pool
A quiet centerpiece built into the courtyard itself, rather than tucked behind it.
The Pavilion
A glass-roofed space for events and dinners — open to the sky, sheltered from the weather.
The Grounds
Landscaped the way the rest of Almond was chosen: leaf by leaf, colour by colour.
